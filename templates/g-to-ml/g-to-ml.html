How to Convert Grams to Milliliters

To convert grams (mass) to milliliters (volume), you need the density of the substance, measured in grams per milliliter ($\mathbf{g/mL}$).

Formula:

Milliliters (mL) = Grams (G) ÷ Density (g/mL)

Example:

Let’s convert 200 grams of cooking oil into milliliters (Density of cooking oil is approximately $\mathbf{0.918\ \text{g/mL}}$):

  • Milliliters = 200 G ÷ 0.918 g/mL
  • Milliliters ≈ 217.865

So, 200 grams of cooking oil is approximately equal to **217.865** milliliters.

Definition Of Gram:

A **gram (g)** is a unit of mass in the metric system. It is commonly used to measure the weight of objects and substances. One gram is equal to $\mathbf{1/1000}$ of a kilogram (kg).

Definition Of Milliliter:

A **milliliter (mL)** is a metric unit of volume equal to one thousandth of a liter ($\mathbf{1/1000\ \text{L}}$). It is the base unit for small metric volume measurements.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is 1 gram always equal to 1 milliliter?

-

A: Only for pure **water** at ($4^{\circ}\text{C}$), where its density is $1.0\ \text{g/mL}$. For any other substance, the weight in grams will result in a different volume in milliliters, depending on its density.

Q: How does the density affect the conversion?

-

A: Density is mass per volume. If a substance is **denser** (like salt, density $\approx 2.16\ \text{g/mL}$), 100 grams of it will occupy a **smaller** volume (fewer milliliters). If a substance is **less dense** (like flour, density $\approx 0.53\ \text{g/mL}$), 100 grams of it will occupy a **larger** volume (more milliliters).
