Liter to KG Conversion:

A liter (ℓ) measures volume, while a kilogram (kg) measures mass. Both represent different units so direct conversions are not possible. To make conversion density of the substance or material is compulsory.

Formula:

Kilograms = Liters × Density

You need to know the density of the substance to apply the formula.

Examples:

  • Convert 1 litre to kg for a substance which has density 1 kg per liter.

Solution:

By using the above formula we will solve this example:

Kilograms = 1 x 1

Kilograms = 1

Where

  • 1 = Liters of the substance
  • 1 = Density of the substance

So, the 1 litre of substance which has a density of 1 kg per liter is equal to 1 kilogram.

  • Convert 5 liter to kg for substance which have density of 1.38 kg per liter

Solution:

By using the above formula we will solve this example:

Kilograms = 5 x 1.38

Kilograms = 6.90

Where;

  • 5 = Liters of the substance
  • 1.38 = Density of the substance

So, the 5 litre of substance which has a density of 1.38 kg per liter is equal to 6.90 kilogram.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can 1 liter always equal 1 kilogram?

-

A: No. Only pure water at standard conditions (4°C) has 1 liter = 1 kilogram. Other liquids, like milk, oil, or honey, have different densities, so their weights will vary.

Q: Is the L to KG converter of mltomg.info accurate for all substances?

-

A: Yes, as long as the correct density of the substance is used. Since density can change with temperature, results are approximate in some cases. But our team make sure of the accuracy of this converter with the help of experts.
