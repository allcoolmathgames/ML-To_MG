How to Convert Fluid Ounces to Milliliters (and other metric units)

The conversion depends on whether you are using the US Fluid Ounce or the UK/Imperial Fluid Ounce.

Conversion Factors:

  • $\mathbf{1\ \text{US fl oz}} \approx \mathbf{29.5735\ \text{mL}}$
  • $\mathbf{1\ \text{UK fl oz}} \approx \mathbf{28.4131\ \text{mL}}$

Formula (Ounces to Milliliters):

Metric Unit Conversions:

  • Centiliters (cL) = mL / 10
  • Liters (L) = mL / 1000

Definition Of Fluid Ounce:

A **fluid ounce (fl oz)** is an imperial and US customary unit of volume. It is important to distinguish between the two: the **US Fluid Ounce** is commonly used in the United States and is roughly $\mathbf{29.57\ \text{mL}}$, while the slightly smaller **UK/Imperial Fluid Ounce** is used in the UK and Canada and is approximately $\mathbf{28.41\ \text{mL}}$.

Definition Of Milliliter:

A **milliliter (mL)** is a metric unit of volume equal to one thousandth of a liter ($\mathbf{1/1000\ \text{L}}$). It is the base unit for small metric volume measurements.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Why are US and UK fluid ounces different?

-

A: The difference stems from historical variations in the definition of the base volume units (gallons). The US fluid ounce is based on the US wine gallon, while the UK/Imperial fluid ounce is based on the Imperial gallon.

Q: What is the relationship between mL, cL, and L?

-

A: These are all metric units of volume:

  • 1 Liter (L) = 100 Centiliters (cL)
  • 1 Liter (L) = 1000 Milliliters (mL)
  • 1 Centiliter (cL) = 10 Milliliters (mL)
