How to Convert Milliliters to Fluid Ounces

Converting milliliters to fluid ounces is a simple volume conversion. The type of substance (water, oil, etc.) does not matter.

Definition Of Milliliter:

A **milliliter (mL)** is a metric unit of volume equal to one thousandth of a liter ($\mathbf{1/1000\ \text{L}}$). It is commonly used for measuring small amounts of liquid, especially in cooking and science.

Definition Of Fluid Ounce:

A **fluid ounce (fl oz)** is an imperial and US customary unit of volume. In the US Customary system, one fluid ounce is exactly $\mathbf{1/128}$ of a US gallon, which is approximately **$29.5735\ \text{mL}$**.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Do I need to know the density to convert mL to fl oz?

-

A: No. Converting milliliters to fluid ounces is a direct **volume-to-volume** conversion. The density of the substance (like water or oil) is only needed when converting between **volume** (mL, L, fl oz) and **mass** (g, kg, oz, lb).

Q: Is a US Fluid Ounce different from an Imperial Fluid Ounce?

-

A: Yes. A US fluid ounce is approximately **$29.57\ \text{mL}$**, whereas an Imperial (UK/Canadian) fluid ounce is slightly larger at approximately **$28.41\ \text{mL}$**. This converter uses the more common US Fluid Ounce.
