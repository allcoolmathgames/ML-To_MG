Definition of Teaspoon:

A teaspoon (tsp) is a unit of volume most commonly used in cooking recipes and for dosing medicine.

A US customary teaspoon is officially defined as **4.92892 milliliters (mL)**.

In many contexts, a rounded value of 5 mL is often used for approximation, especially in pharmaceutical dosing.

Definition of Milligram:

A milligram (mg) is a unit of mass in the metric system equal to one-thousandth of a gram (0.001 g).

1 milligram is equal to 0.000001 kilograms, or 0.000035274 ounces.

The milligram is often used in medicine and for measuring very small amounts of ingredients in cooking and science.

How to Convert Teaspoons to Milligrams

To convert measurement from tsp to milligrams, you must know the density of the substance, because teaspoons measure volume, while milligrams measure mass.

Formula:

Mass (mg) = Volume (tsp) √ó Density (g/mL) √ó 4928.92

Where 4928.92 is the factor to convert Density in g/mL to mg/tsp (4928.92 = 1000 mg/g √ó 4.92892 mL/tsp).

What is 2 teaspoons in milligrams for a substance which has a density of 1.00 g/mL (Water)?

Mass (mg) = 2 tsp √ó 1.00 g/mL √ó 4928.92

2 tsp of substance with density **1.00 g/mL** is equal to **9857.84** milligrams.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How many milligrams are in one teaspoon of water?

-

A: One US teaspoon of pure water (density $\approx 1.00\ \text{g/mL}$) is equal to approximately **4928.92 milligrams** (or about $4.93\ \text{grams}$).

Q: Can I use the same conversion factor for all liquids?

-

A: No, you cannot. Liquids and dry goods have different densities. For example, 1 tsp of water has a different mass than 1 tsp of cooking oil because oil has a lower density (around $0.92\ \text{g/mL}$).
