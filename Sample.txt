            <h2>Liter to KG Conversion:</h2>
            <p>A liter (в„“) measures volume, while a kilogram (kg) measures mass. Both represent different units so direct conversions are not possible. To make conversion density of the substance or material is compulsory.</p>
            <h3>Formula:</h3>
            <p style="text-align: center; color: red;"> Kilograms = Liters Г— Density</p>
            <p>You need to know the density of the substance to apply the formula.</p>
            <h4>Examples:</h4>
            <ul>
            <li>Convert 1 litre to kg for a substance which has density 1 kg per liter.</li>
            </ul>
            <p><b>Solution:</b></p>
            <p>By using the above formula we will solve this example:</p>
            <p style="text-align: center; color: red;"> Kilograms = 1 x 1</p>
            <p style="text-align: center; color: red;"> Kilograms = 1</p>
            <p>Where</p>
            <ul>
                <li>1 = Liters of the substance</li>
                <li>1 = Density of the substance</li>
            </ul>
            <p>So, the 1 litre of substance which has a density of 1 kg per liter is equal to 1 kilogram. </p>
            <ul>
                <li>Convert 5 liter to kg for substance which have density of 1.38 kg per liter</li>
            
            </ul>
            <p><b>Solution:</b></p>
            <p>By using the above formula we will solve this example:</p>
            <p style="text-align: center; color: red;"> Kilograms = 5 x 1.38</p>
            <p style="text-align: center; color: red;"> Kilograms = 6.90</p>
            <p>Where;</p>
            <ul>
                <li>5 = Liters of the substance</li>
                <li>1.38 = Density of the substance</li>
            </ul>
            <p>So, the 5 litre of substance which has a density of 1.38 kg per liter is equal to 6.90 kilogram. </p>
